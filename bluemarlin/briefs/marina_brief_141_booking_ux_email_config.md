# BRIEF 141 — Booking UX + Email Config: Confirmation Wording, Flow Pacing, Email Routing
**Status:** Draft | **Files:** `agents/social/social_agent.py`, `agents/marina/marina_agent.py`, `config/client.json` | **Depends on:** Brief 140 | **Blocks:** None

## Context

Three fixes from Benson's Phase 1 review:

1. **Booking confirmation wording** — Marina says "Want me to go ahead and book this?" before checking availability. The word "book" implies finality. If availability check fails, the customer feels jerked around. Should be "let me check if that's available" vibe.

2. **Booking flow too fast** — On all channels, Marina jumps straight into field collection. Customer says "I want to book sunset cruise" → Marina immediately asks for date and guests. No info about the service, no warmth, just a transaction. Should give info about the service first (what's included, schedule), then naturally flow into collecting details.

3. **Client email config** — `business.email` is `info@bluefinncharters.com` (the business owner's email). But for the demo, the customer-facing inbox is `hello@wetakeyourjob.com` (where Marina reads from). The DM agent redirects bookings to `business.email` which is the owner's email, not Marina's inbox. Need a separate field for the customer-facing contact email.

## Why This Approach

**Fix 1:** Change the hardcoded booking summary string in `_build_booking_summary()` (social_agent.py line 86). The summary is generated by Python, not Claude. Change "Want me to go ahead and book this?" to "Want me to check availability and hold a spot for you?" This sets the right expectation — we're checking, not booking.

Also update the action context prompt (line 93-106) to match — the customer is replying to an availability check, not a booking confirmation.

**Fix 2:** Add a BOOKING PACING section to Marina's prompt in marina_agent.py. When a customer first mentions wanting to book, Marina should give a brief summary of the service (what's included, schedule, key details from the service data) before asking for booking details. Not a wall of text — just enough info to feel professional and helpful. This applies to all channels.

**Fix 3:** Add `business.booking_email` to client.json — the email customers should use to book. For the demo: `hello@wetakeyourjob.com`. The DM agent uses this for redirect. `business.email` stays as the business owner's email (used for escalations). `business.support_email` stays as the escalation destination.
